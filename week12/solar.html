Solar

First, I feel the need to stress the importance of having the right equipment for looking at the Sun. While many people are quite capable of safely modifying and using modified solar telescopes and observation equipment, I choose to err on the side of caution. Therefore, I obtained a telescope that is designed for safe solar observation. I started out with a Lunt 40mm Dedicated Hydrogen-Alpha Solar scope, but found that it wasn't quite where I wanted to be, so I upgraded to the Lunt 60mm Universal.

The Sun is the one object in the sky we don’t usually look at.

But once a person sees it, it’s hard not to look. With a Hydrogen-Alpha scope, you only see about 0.01% of the visible light that is generated by the sun. But it’s a magical 0.01%, and is by far the most interesting and entertaining emission line from the Sun. The view is different every time you look. There are events such as CMTs (Coronal Mass Ejections) and flares which are becoming more frequent. They are by far the most awe inspiring events that can be viewed thru a Solar scope in real time. The surface seems to be a swirling mass of fire, ever changing and very dynamic as it’s flows in real time.

A Solar Telescope is a completely different animal to its cousin, the nighttime scope. A Solar Telescope is a multi-filter system that requires an engineered precision balance of aperture, etalon size and performance, f-ratio, etalon placement, safety filters, and out of band blocking. It is not a secret that the price of a Solar Systems increases dramatically based on the size of the etalon paired to the system. Nighttime scopes are light buckets. They are being used to try and resolve faint and distant objects. The larger the aperture you can utilize given the seeing conditions, the more detail you can resolve on faint objects.

My Solar Equipment

This is my Lunt 60mm Universal Telescope. This is the smallest scope in Lunts line of Universal scopes, and is capable of both solar and nighttime veiwing, I most likely will use it only for solar. It has the Hydrogen Alpha blocking filter B600, which is a 2" diagonal. It has a Rack and Pinon focuser. Someday, I plan to upgrade this scope with a Lunt LS 60FHa Double stack unit, which will just make already wonderful views even better.The focal ratio is f/7 at a focal lengh of 420mm. It is usually mounted on my Celestron Advanced VX mount, which is the lighter of my two main EQ mounts (I also have a Sky-Watcher EQ6-R Pro, which tends to be more stable, but way heavier). This image was taken with an ZWO ASI174mm Planetary camera, and processed with Astropixel software. I purchased both Lunt scopes through Mike at OC Telescopes.
