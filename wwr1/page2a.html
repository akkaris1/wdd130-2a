Yampa River Rafting

Yampa River rafting is special because the Yampa is WILD. This is the last river in the Colorado River system that is not regulated by a dam. Despite continued attempts by the Bureau of Reclamation and Congress, the only major dam ever constructed on the Yampa River was built by Mother Nature. And even that was temporary.

On June 9th, 1965, a massive flash flood and debris flow carried thousands of tons of rock down Warm Springs Draw, completely blocking the river channel. The river slowed upstream of the obstruction, and the current snarled and boiled and pushed against the debris. It took only 24 hours for the river to breach the natural dam, leaving in its wake Warm Springs Rapid, rated as one of the 10 biggest drops in the country. Dee Holladay was one of the first to run the newly formed rapid..

Gates of Lodore

Rafting through the Gates of Lodore is like entering an enchanted wonderland. Bighorn sheep, bears, osprey, eagles, and other wildlife roam the river banks, and history comes to life through rapids and rock art. Deep in the heart of Dinosaur National Monument, Lodore Canyon rafting trips are great for families and first-time river runners. .

Gates of Lodore rafting trips carry you over some thrilling rapids, including Disaster Falls and Hells Half Mile, as well as dozens of smaller rapids that are great fun to paddle inflatable kayaks through. Hike to secret waterfalls. swimming holes, and thousand-year-old rock art left behind by some of the canyon’s original inhabitants.

West Water Canyon

If you’re looking for a river rafting trip that has it all, but you only have a weekend to get away, look no further than our trip through Ruby and Westwater Canyons on the Colorado River! Get a little taste of everything a river trip has to offer. Spend a weekend discovering the majesty of the Colorado River in the canyon that some call a miniature Grand Canyon.
